Scientific Notation
Any number can be written as x × 10ʸ where 1 ≤ x < 10. This makes huge and tiny numbers easy to read and compare — the exponent tells you the scale at a glance.

Rule: Move the decimal point until you have a number between 1 and 10. Each move left = exponent +1. Each move right = exponent −1.

The exponent is the most important part. To compare two numbers in scientific notation, first compare their exponents. The one with the larger exponent is much bigger — changing the exponent by 1 multiplies the number by 10, while changing the coefficient from 1 to 9 only multiplies it by 9.
